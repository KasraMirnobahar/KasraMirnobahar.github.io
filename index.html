Current project

Research spotlight

PhD Research Proposal · 2026

Person-Specific Digital Phenotyping of Social Media Use and Digital Well-Being

Despite growing public concern, research on social media and mental health remains inconsistent — largely because it treats users as a homogeneous group and relies on crude screen-time metrics. This project proposes a multi-modal approach integrating data donation (TikTok behavioural logs via GDPR data portability), Ecological Momentary Assessment through the How We Feel platform, and computational behavioural metrics (persistence, checking frequency, content exploration, temporal adaptation) to build person-specific affective-behavioural profiles. Dynamic Structural Equation Modelling (DSEM) and data-driven clustering (GIMME) will identify individual-level patterns linking mood to digital engagement — informing personalised interventions rather than blanket policy restrictions.
